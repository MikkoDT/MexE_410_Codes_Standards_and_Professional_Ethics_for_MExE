## 📌 The Scenario
You are the lead engineering team at a Mechatronics startup. Your group has conceptualized a novel mechatronic product or service (e.g., an autonomous warehouse drone, an AI-driven surgical assist tool, an automated petrochemical pipeline crawler). 

Before this product can legally enter the global market, it must pass a rigorous third-party ISO/IEC accreditation audit to ensure it is safe, ethical, and reliable. 

To execute this, the class will engage in **Cross-Auditing**. Every group will play two roles simultaneously:
1. **The Innovators:** Defending your own product's safety and standards.
2. **The ISO Auditors:** Inspecting the safety and standards of *another* group's product.

*(Example: Group 1 audits Group 2. Group 2 audits Group 3. Group 3 audits Group 1.)*

---

## 🛠️ The 4-Phase Accreditation Process

### Phase 1: Product Development & Standards Mapping (Submission Deadline)
**Role:** The Innovators  
Invent a unique mechatronic product. You are not building a physical prototype; you are engineering the technical documentation. Submit a **Technical Dossier** containing:
* **Product Description:** What it does, target industry, and core mechanical/electrical components.
* **Safety Architecture:** Explicitly define the emergency stops, fail-safes, and thermal/kinematic limits.
* **Standards Mapping:** Identify at least **two** specific ISO/IEC standards that apply to your product (e.g., ISO 12100 for Safety of Machinery, ISO 10218 for Robotics) and explain exactly how your product complies with them.

### Phase 2: The Dossier Exchange & Internal Review
**Role:** The ISO Auditors  
Immediately after the submission deadline, the lecturer will facilitate a "Dossier Exchange." Your group will receive the Technical Dossier of another group.
* **The Mission:** Review their documentation. As professional ISO Auditors, hunt for the physical safety gaps, logical errors, and ethical risks they failed to consider.
* **The Deliverable:** Prepare an **Audit Checklist** consisting of 5 highly technical, probing questions you will ask them during the live presentation to test their safety architecture.

### Phase 3: The Live Accreditation Audit (Presentation Date)
**Role:** Both Innovators and Auditors  
This is a live, time-limited defense.
* **The Pitch (5 mins):** The Innovators present their product and explain why it meets international standards.
* **The Interrogation (5-10 mins):** The assigned ISO Auditors take the floor. They use their prepared Audit Checklist to cross-examine the Innovators live. The Innovators must professionally defend their engineering logic. 

### Phase 4: Non-Conformance & CAPA Resolution
**Role:** Both Innovators and Auditors  
Following the live presentation, finalize the documentation:
* **The NCR (From Auditors):** The auditing group issues a formal **Non-Conformance Report (NCR)** to the Innovators, listing the major standards violations or logic flaws discovered during the presentation.
* **The CAPA (From Innovators):** The innovating group must submit a **Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA)** plan. Detail exactly how you will re-engineer your product to fix the flaws identified in the NCR and achieve full accreditation.

## 📊 Grading Rubric (100% Total)

**As Innovators (50%):**
* **Technical Dossier (20%):** Accuracy of standards mapping and depth of safety architecture.
* **Live Defense (15%):** Ability to professionally defend engineering choices against the Auditors.
* **CAPA Resolution (15%):** Viability and compliance of the proposed engineering fixes.

**As ISO Auditors (50%):**
* **Audit Checklist (20%):** The technical rigor and relevance of the 5 prepared questions.
* **The Interrogation (15%):** Professionalism, critical thinking, and the ability to spot logic flaws live.
* **The NCR (15%):** Accuracy in citing specific ISO/IEC standards when logging violations.
