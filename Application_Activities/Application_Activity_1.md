## 📌 The Scenario

Your engineering firm has received translated operation manuals from international subsidiaries. An internal audit suspects that **one lethal engineering logic error** has been mistranslated or altered in each document. 

Your group must identify and correct this hazard before the machinery is deployed. Failure to do so will result in catastrophic mechanical failure, facility destruction, or loss of life.

---

## 🛠️ Instructions

You will be assigned one foreign-language engineering excerpt corresponding to a specific industrial sector (e.g., Heavy Manufacturing, Medical Robotics, Petrochemical). 

You are strictly prohibited from using basic translation tools to blindly "guess" the error. You must conduct a professional engineering audit using a Hybrid AI Approach.

---

## 👥 Team Member Responsibilities & Deliverables

Divide your group into three specific roles. Each member has distinct deliverables.

### 1. Project Engineer
**Role:** Documentation and Version Control
* **Repository Setup:** Create a private GitHub repository for the group project and add the lecturer as a collaborator.
* **Naming Convention:** Title the repository exactly as follows:  
  `MexE410_Industrial_Robotics_International_Compliance_Standards_Section_Group#_ApplicationActivity1_2026`
* **Documentation:** Write the final output into this `README.md` file. You must clearly document the following:
  * **Target Language:** The assigned language of your excerpt.
  * **The Raw Translation:** The direct English output from DeepL/Google.
  * **The Fatal Flaw:** Identify the exact sabotaged term (e.g., "normally open" vs "normally closed").
  * **The Engineering Consequence:** Provide a brief, highly technical explanation of exactly *why* this error would cause a physical catastrophe on the factory floor.
  * **The Corrected Code/Text:** The repaired sentence demonstrating proper compliance.

### 2. Quality Assurance (QA) Engineer
**Role:** Hybrid AI Auditing  
Execute the Audit Workflow using the following exact steps:
1. **The Baseline Translation:** Run your assigned foreign text through DeepL (or Google Translate for unsupported languages like Urdu, Tagalog, or Arabic) to obtain a raw, literal English baseline.
2. **AI Contextual Analysis:** Paste your baseline translation into a Large Language Model (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, or Gemini). 
3. **The Interrogation:** Prompt the AI as a QA Engineer using this exact text:
   > "Act as a Mechatronics QA Auditor. Analyze this translated procedure. Does the mechanical logic ensure physical safety and comply with ISO standards? Flag any relay states, kinematic bounds, or thermal limits that could cause a catastrophic failure. Present your findings in a maximum of 5 concise bullet points."
4. **The Isolation:** Identify the exact logic and standards errors using this exact prompt:
   > "Based on your previous analysis, pinpoint the exact engineering logic error and identify any specific ISO/IEC standard violations. List these specific errors in a maximum of 5 concise bullet points. Then, provide the necessary engineering corrections to resolve these hazards and ensure compliance, also formatted as a maximum of 5 concise bullet points."

*Provide the final isolated error to the Project Engineer for documentation.*

### 3. Project Leader
**Role:** Final Review and Presentation  
Prepare a strict **10-minute presentation** summarizing the audit for the class. Your presentation must clearly flow through the QA Engineer's findings.

**Presentation Content Requirements:**
1. **System Overview:** Briefly introduce your assigned industrial sector, the specific machinery, and the target language of the manual.
2. **The Audit Methodology:** Briefly explain the Hybrid AI process your group used (baseline translation and the prompting strategy).
3. **Initial Safety Flags:** Present the results from the QA Engineer's *Interrogation* phase. Share the 5 concise bullets highlighting the relay states, kinematic bounds, or thermal limits that threatened physical safety.
4. **The Fatal Flaw & ISO/IEC Violations:** Present the results from the QA Engineer's *Isolation* phase. Clearly define the exact engineering logic error and explicitly name the specific international standards (ISO/IEC) that were violated.
5. **Engineering Corrections:** Present the 5 concise bullet points detailing the necessary engineering fixes required to resolve the hazard and bring the machinery back into compliance.
6. **Conclusion:** Summarize the real-world engineering consequences—what would have physically happened on the factory floor if your team had not caught this translation error? End with a brief reflection on why rigorous QA auditing of technical documentation is critical in Mechatronics.
