# Final Project: The Mechatronics Ethics & Standards Tabletop Game

**Institution:** Batangas State University – The National Engineering University  
**Course:** MEXE 410: Codes, Standards, and Professional Ethics for MExE  
**Target:** 3rd Year BS Mechatronics Engineering  

---

## 📌 Project Overview
Engineering codes, safety standards, and professional ethics are not just theories; they are active decisions made every day in the industry. For your final project, your group will design, build, and test an original tabletop game (a board game or a card game) that simulates the real-world application of MEXE 410 principles.

Your game must be playable by **2 to 4 players** and must force the players to navigate ethical dilemmas, comply with international standards, and manage engineering risks within a specific Mechatronics sub-field.

---

## 🎲 Topic Assignment
During class, the lecturer will use a "Wheel of Names" to randomly assign each group one of the following specialized industry topics. Your game's theme, hazards, and standards must be strictly built around this assigned topic:

* Mechatronics Engineering in Research, Design and Development
* Mechatronics Engineering in Manufacturing and Process Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Robotics and AI
* Mechatronics Engineering in Mechanical Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Electronics Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Electrical Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Control Systems Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Computer Science and Software Engineering
* Mechatronics Engineering in Business
* Mechatronics Engineering in Academe

---

## 🛠️ Game Design Requirements
* **Player Count:** The game must strictly support 2, 3, or 4 players.
* **Educational Integration:** The core mechanics must involve applying ISO/IEC standards, local codes, or solving ethical conflicts. For example, players might lose points for bypassing a safety code or win by successfully certifying an industrial tool.
* **Game Mechanics:** You are free to design any system that emphasizes precision and industrial utility. You might use custom dice, such as a primary die displaying faces like "1/1, 2/2, 3/3" where the first number dictates a base compliance score and the second half dictates an integration score. Alternatively, you could develop card mechanics that allow players to chain multiple consecutive engineering actions or replicate successful component designs on the board to build a specialized engine.

## 📂 Deliverables & Submission Process

### 1. The GitHub Repository (Documentation)
Create a new GitHub repository named `Final_MEXE410_Section_Group#_2026`. This repository must contain:
* **The Rulebook:** A detailed `README.md` explaining the objective, setup, turn order, and win conditions of your game.
* **The Developer Log:** A section in the `README.md` documenting your creation process, including photos of your prototype development.
* **The Video Playtest:** A link (YouTube or Google Drive) to a recorded video of your group doing a live test run of the game, proving the mechanics work.

### 2. The Physical Game (Presentation Day)
You must bring the fully completed, physical version of your board game or card game to class. Ensure you have all necessary pieces, cards, dice, and a printed rulebook. 

### 3. The Live Playtest Evaluation
Presentation day will not be a standard slideshow. We will set up dedicated playtest stations. Your group will act as the "Game Masters." You must teach and run a live game session where the lecturer, visiting faculty members, or students from other groups sit down and play your game.
